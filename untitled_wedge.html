    Untitled Wedge

    I competed with my friend Ian KSU Combat Robotics Tournament (March 22, 9am-5pm, Timken Atrium). For our robot we called it "Untitled Wedge", we built our bot with the intention of knowing zero battle bot engineering. The design classified as a Control-Bot, as it has no weapon. Knowing our competitors we wanted to take advantage of the gaps between the bottom of their bots and the floor. This allowed us to become a ramp where we could redirect the opponent's movement to cause their weapon to not hit our bot and/or flip them upside-down, where they couldn't move after. Our first match was against the over winner, so we ending up losing. However we stood our ground and drew it to time, while others ended it K-O. The second match was as strong showing of control and aggression, however the driver got too aggressive and got the bot stuck in the gap between the wall and floor, causing us to lose by K-O. For the last match in the bracket was a forfeit from our opponent due to the winner destroying them to disrepair. But that wasn't the last battle of us that day, at the end we all wanted a brawl, where all working bots would battle at the same time. After the brawl the remaining bot that could move was the winning bot (Katana) and us.
