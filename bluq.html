    BluQ Hack-Dearborn 2025

    I participated with my friends from Kent State, Austin, Ian, and Mason at the Hack-Dearborn 2025 Hackathon. Our project BluQ is a project where we configure a mesh-network using BlueTooth where we can enable various forms of network communication: messaging, voice-call, video, etc. We used raspberry-pi devices to act as our nodes within our network and created a client program that allows a user to connect to the closest node and dynamically route their messages throughout the network. Having this system dynamic allows us if a node were to go down for any reason we provide redudancy where the client will not suffer any delays due to a disabled node. Our original design we wanted to use Free-Wili devices with BlueTooth modules as our nodes but we unfortunately ran into many difficulties implementing our idea on these devices.
